# A.8.6 Capacity Management

## Objective

Ensure IT resources are monitored, planned, and managed to meet current and future business requirements without compromising performance or security.

## Implementation Guidance

- Document capacity management policies aligned with business needs.
- Monitor system performance using tools (e.g., Nagios, SolarWinds, Zabbix).
- Define thresholds and automated alerts.
- Use forecasting models to anticipate growth or seasonal peaks.
- Integrate capacity planning into change management and project planning.
- Include capacity considerations in new projects and major changes.

## Audit Criteria

- Are capacity management policies documented, approved, and reviewed?
- Are monitoring tools configured and actively used?
- Are performance logs retained and analyzed?
- Are capacity forecasts documented and updated?
- Are threshold breaches tracked, investigated, and resolved?

## Evidence Examples

- Monitoring dashboards and screenshots
- Capacity planning reports
- Performance logs
- Incident tickets related to performance or capacity
- Change requests for scaling infrastructure

## Typical Findings

- No formal capacity planning process
- Alerts not configured or not reviewed
- Forecasting not performed or not documented
- Reactive scaling after incidents instead of proactive planning

## Maturity Indicators

- Level 1: Ad-hoc monitoring, no formal planning
- Level 2: Basic monitoring with manual reviews
- Level 3: Defined capacity management process and documented forecasts
- Level 4: Automated monitoring, alerting, and trend analysis
- Level 5: Predictive analytics integrated with business and financial planning

## Related Controls

- A.5.23 Information Security in Use of Cloud Services

**Benefits:** Reduces performance incidents, supports business growth, and prevents security risks caused by overloaded systems.
