# A.8.6 Capacity Management

## Objective

Ensure IT resources are monitored, planned, and managed to meet current and future business requirements without compromising performance or security.

## Implementation Guidance

- Document capacity management policies aligned with business needs.  
- Monitor system performance using tools (e.g., Nagios, SolarWinds, Zabbix).  
- Define thresholds and automated alerts.  
- Use forecasting models to anticipate growth or seasonal peaks.  
- Integrate capacity planning into change management and project planning.  

## Audit Criteria

- Are policies documented, approved, and reviewed?  
- Are monitoring tools configured and actively used?  
- Are performance logs retained and analyzed?  
- Are capacity forecasts documented?  
- Are threshold breaches tracked and resolved?  

## Evidence Examples

- Monitoring dashboards  
- Capacity planning reports  
- Performance logs  
- Incident tickets  
- Change requests for scaling  

## Typical Findings

- No formal capacity planning process  
- Alerts not configured  
- Forecasting not performed  
- Reactive rather than proactive scaling  

## Maturity Indicators

- Level 1: Ad‑hoc monitoring  
- Level 2: Basic monitoring  
- Level 3: Defined process  
- Level 4: Automated monitoring and forecasting  
- Level 5: Predictive analytics integrated with business strategy  

## Related Controls

- A.5.23 Information Security in Development  
- A.8.9 Configuration Management  

**Benefits:** Prevents downtime, ensures smooth operations, and avoids overloading systems that could lead to vulnerabilities.
