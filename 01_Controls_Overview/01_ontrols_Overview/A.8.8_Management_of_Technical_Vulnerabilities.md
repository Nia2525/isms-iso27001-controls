# A.8.8 Management of Technical Vulnerabilities


## Objective

Identify, assess, prioritize, and remediate vulnerabilities in a timely and risk‑based manner.


## Implementation Guidance

•	Conduct regular vulnerability scans (Nessus, Qualys, OpenVAS).
•	Maintain a documented patch management process.
•	Prioritize vulnerabilities based on CVSS, asset criticality, and business impact.
•	Track remediation through a ticketing system.
•	Define patching SLAs (e.g., critical within 7 days).


## Audit Criteria

•	Is there a documented vulnerability management policy?
•	Are scans performed regularly and comprehensively?
•	Are vulnerabilities risk‑rated and prioritized?
•	Are patches deployed within SLAs?
•	Are exceptions documented and approved?


## Evidence Examples

•	Scan reports
•	Patch logs
•	Remediation tickets
•	Risk acceptance forms
•	Asset inventory


## Typical Findings

•	Missing scans for cloud or remote assets
•	Critical vulnerabilities open beyond SLA
•	No correlation between asset inventory and scan scope
•	No remediation tracking


## Maturity Indicators

•	Level 1: Reactive patching
•	Level 2: Regular scanning
•	Level 3: SLA‑based remediation
•	Level 4: Automated patching
•	Level 5: Continuous monitoring


## Related Controls

**Benefits:** Minimizes exposure to attacks and ensures compliance with regulatory requirements.
